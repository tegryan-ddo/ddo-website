DevOps, we're doing it wrong (still)

By on 6 October 2018

DevOps - We need to think “change management”

Simply put, SaaS software development is a continuous excercise in change management. You have your development team creating as many changes as they can, and your leads and managers making sure those changes are generally aligned to the larger context of the organization by reviewing and approving them. I think it’s a safe statement to say that any CTO would love %100 of the development team’s time be dedicated to just that - to solving “business problems” as it’s commonly called. However, the world of software development comes with a very large overhead, even before the movement to join development and operations in DevOps. Nowadays, developers are expected to own much more of the entire delivery solution Therefore they end up spending time building pipelines, monitoring, logging, troubleshooting, and all sorts of overhead features.

Git as change management

So how do we gain the time back for developers and especially the team leads? The key is focusing on change management. Typically in today’s world most SaaS development shops use Git, or at least some form of version control.
